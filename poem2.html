A Boy

Today I saw a little boy
That drowned as he fled a war
He was one of the many victims
That could never walk on this shore

His crime was that he was born
The wrong side of a land
And that his parents fled a country
And took him by the hand

An ad says what should we do?
As a country throw them out?
Lock the doors and hide the keys?
Let nature work it out?

If I even have a voice
My voice would be to say
Who are you to decide
Who can live here and who can stay

The shores are filing, stacking up
With bodies from the sea
People who are scared and fleeing
People just like you and me

So look into your loved ones eyes
And tell me honest and true
Would you not ask for shelter somewhere
If it was your loved one and if it was you?

Would you not die inside
If your baby ends up dead
Drowned because of the great big sea
How many tears would you have shed?

A long life up ahead
He went towards the wrong safety
And thus he ended up dead
